College of Engineering

CoE Goal

Produce globally competitive and value laden professionals/entrepreneurs who can innovatate/create social, economic, environmental and industrial impact through sustainable research and community services.

CoE Objectives

      1. Equip students with thorough understanding of the concepts and applications of scientific theories necessary in the practice of chosen profession.
      2. Capacitate students to grow and develop into a holistic professional equipped woth 21st century skills who can provide solutions to society and communinity problems.
      3. Provide a word-class teaching and learning environment that maximizes the students learning potential to support creativity and innovations in research and product development.
      4. Inculcate values of honesty, integrity and social accountability.

Blueprint of Excellence: The College of Engineering Through the Years

As the country's National Engineering University, the College of Engineering at Batangas State University stands as a testament to the Philippines' pursuit of excellence in innovation, with a rich history marked by continuous growth and transformative development.

The college traces its roots back to 1985, when it was established as the Engineering Department, laying the foundation for engineering education at the university. In 1992, it was renamed simply as Engineering, signifying its emergence as a distinct academic unit. By 1997, with the introduction of architecture programs, the college expanded and became the College of Engineering and Architecture (CEA).

In 2000, the institution broadened its academic portfolio to include fine arts, adopting the name College of Engineering, Architecture and Fine Arts (CEAFA). This growth continued in 2006 with the inclusion of computing sciences, prompting another renaming as the College of Engineering, Architecture, Fine Arts, and Computing Sciences (CEAFACS).

However, in 2013, the college returned to the title College of Engineering, Architecture and Fine Arts (CEAFA), reflecting a refined focus on its core disciplines. Most recently, in 2022—following Batangas State University's designation as the Philippines' National Engineering University—the college embraced a streamlined identity as the College of Engineering (COE), reinforcing its leadership role in shaping the future of engineering education in the country.

This dynamic evolution underscores the college's adaptability, resilience, and steadfast commitment to addressing the ever-changing educational needs of its students and the broader community.
